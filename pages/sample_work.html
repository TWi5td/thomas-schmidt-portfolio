Research Papers

ECE 4700: Final Project – Comparative Study of Classical Control Methods

This paper critically examines the limitations of the Routh-Hurwitz criterion and root locus technique in classical control theory. It compares their computational effort, information content, and practical shortcomings, using a DC motor position control example. The study highlights why modern robust control methods (e.g., H∞ and LQR) are essential for real-world applications.

High School Senior Paper – Biological Enhancements and Society

This argumentative paper examines the ethical and societal implications of biological enhancements to the human body. It explores three main concerns: the integrity of athletic competition in the face of performance-enhancing drugs, the environmental impact of life extension technologies on overpopulation, and the psychological effects of biological modifications. The paper argues that while enhancement technologies offer benefits, their widespread use poses significant risks to fairness, sustainability, and human identity.

Capstone Project – SCADA Simulation Platform

Designed and implemented a low-cost SCADA simulation platform using Raspberry Pi units to emulate substation IEDs with Modbus and DNP3 protocols. Supports SD-card configuration, real-time Ethernet and serial communication, and simulation of binary and analog points. Developed for integration with SEL RTACs for educational and testing scenarios.

This Project was funded by POWER Engineers, acquired by WSP.

Research Papers

ECE 4700: Final Project – Comparative Study of Classical Control Methods

This paper critically examines the limitations of the Routh-Hurwitz criterion and root locus technique in classical control theory. It compares their computational effort, information content, and practical shortcomings, using a DC motor position control example. The study highlights why modern robust control methods (e.g., H∞ and LQR) are essential for real-world applications.

High School Senior Paper – Biological Enhancements and Society

This argumentative paper examines the ethical and societal implications of biological enhancements to the human body. It explores three main concerns: the integrity of athletic competition in the face of performance-enhancing drugs, the environmental impact of life extension technologies on overpopulation, and the psychological effects of biological modifications. The paper argues that while enhancement technologies offer benefits, their widespread use poses significant risks to fairness, sustainability, and human identity.

Boy Scout of America Eagle Scout Project, Finucane Announcers Box (September 8th, 2019)

For my Eagle Scout Project, completed on September 8th, 2019, I led the planning and construction of the Finucane Announcers Box—a project that challenged and strengthened my leadership abilities at every stage. I was responsible for developing the full project plan, including the design layout, construction approach, and a detailed Bill of Materials to ensure accurate budgeting and procurement. Throughout the build, I coordinated and guided teams of volunteers, assigning tasks based on skill sets, maintaining safety, and keeping the project on schedule. Managing a diverse group of helpers required clear communication, adaptability, and problem‑solving, ultimately teaching me how to lead effectively while turning a community need into a lasting improvement.

This project was funded by Kootenai Girls Softball Association (KGSA)
